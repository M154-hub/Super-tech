Environmental Health Awareness

What is Environmental Health?

Environmental health is the branch of public health concerned with all aspects of the natural and built environment affecting human health. It focuses on how environmental factors such as air, water, soil, and living conditions influence overall well-being.

Air Pollution

Air pollution refers to harmful substances in the atmosphere that can cause health issues such as respiratory problems and heart disease. Reducing emissions from vehicles and industries can help combat air pollution.

Water Quality

Access to clean and safe drinking water is essential for good health. Contaminated water can lead to diseases such as cholera and dysentery. Proper sanitation and treatment processes are critical to maintaining water quality.

How to Protect the Environment

  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle materials.
  • Conserve water and energy.
  • Limit the use of plastic products.
  • Support policies that promote clean energy and reduce pollution.

Stay informed. Protect our planet for future generations.
